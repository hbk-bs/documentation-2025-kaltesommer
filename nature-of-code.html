In this project, I explored how p5.js can be used to simulate natural patterns. During the course, most students joined an excursion to Amrum to study patterns, behaviors, and systems in nature, such as flocking, wave dynamics, plant growth, and erosion. My initial idea was an interactive audio website where users could locate moving animals in darkness by clicking with a spotlight cursor, and after finding an animal, the next sound would play and a moving image of the next animal would appear on the screen. Since this concept did not directly address natural patterns, I set it aside for a future project. Instead, I focused on visualizing tree rings, a continuous pattern that persists until a tree dies. I developed a blog-based HTML site where I explained the animation process and concept. Through this, I learned how to design a more engaging website and how to translate natural structures into code-based visualizations.
